Please Make This, Field Notes

If you’re anything like me, you require two tools to design a website—a mechanical pencil and a Field Notes graphed notebook. This was all fine and dandy until I read Zeldman’s Designing with Web Standards Third Edition and saw his funky graph paper on page 347. It’s absolutely perfect for sketching grid-based layouts. There is a double vertical line used to signify the gutter margin used in most grid-based layouts. Then it hit me:

Combine the Field Notes graphed notebook and Zeldman’s graph style then color it web-standards-beanie blue just for the fun of it.
